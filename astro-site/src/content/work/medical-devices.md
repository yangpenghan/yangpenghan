When China's medical device industry faced a critical gap in IEC 62366 compliance, I saw both a problem and an opportunity. Most companies didn't understand what usability engineering meant for their products — let alone how to implement it systematically.

I built the entire process from scratch: standardized evaluation protocols, trained cross-functional teams, and created a framework that could be replicated across different device categories. The result? A complete consulting system that grew to serve 25+ enterprise clients and became the foundation for national industry assessment standards.

The key insight was treating usability not as a checkbox, but as a core engineering discipline — one that requires the same rigor as any other aspect of device development. This philosophy, combined with behavioral science methodology, made our approach uniquely effective.
