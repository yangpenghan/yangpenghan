Traditional behavioral data analysis is slow — sometimes taking weeks to code and analyze a single dataset. I asked: what if we could automate this using multi-agent AI systems?

PsyPhiClaw is my exploration of that question. Built with Python and LLM APIs, it uses a swarm of specialized agents — each handling different aspects of behavioral analysis (movement tracking, physiological signal processing, pattern recognition) — to produce insights in minutes instead of weeks.

The platform is still in its proof-of-concept stage, but the early results are promising. It represents my conviction that the future of behavioral science lies at the intersection of human expertise and AI capability — not as a replacement, but as an amplifier of human analytical power.
