## Background

When China's medical-device industry faced a critical gap in IEC 62366 compliance, I saw both a problem and an opportunity. Most companies didn't understand what usability engineering meant for their products — let alone how to implement it systematically.

## Methodology

I built the full IEC 62366 usability-engineering process from scratch: Use Specification, Task Analysis, Use Risk Analysis, Formative Evaluation, Summative Evaluation, through to Usability Engineering File compilation. Devices served span from startups to listed enterprises, across ventilators, patient monitors, surgical equipment, and IVD diagnostics.

The toolchain is built on the Noldus ecosystem: The Observer XT for behavioral coding and use-error identification, FaceReader for facial-expression analysis, and Viso for multi-angle recording (e.g. a single ventilator-vendor study reached 80+ participants).

## Partnership with TÜV Rheinland

We established an annual framework with TÜV Rheinland to run large-scale usability evaluations (70–80+ participants per batch) and participate in joint audits, helping standardize industry assessment practice.

## Results

Served 25+ medical-device enterprises (including listed companies and provincial device-testing institutes), with 100+ participants across usability tests. This methodology ultimately became the foundation for national industry assessment standards.

## Key insight

Treat usability as a core engineering discipline, not a compliance checkbox — it demands the same rigor as any other aspect of device development. I'm now productizing this capability into a usability-engineering SaaS tool, so the methodology no longer depends on individual experts.
